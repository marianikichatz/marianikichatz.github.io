Academic Experience

MAR 2026 – JUN 2026 MSc Research Project – Spatial Transcriptomics in Cancer Lund University
  • Platform: Analysing head and neck cancer (HNC) tumour microenvironments using in-situ single-cell spatial transcriptomics data from the Xenium platform (10x Genomics).
  • Focus: Investigating localisation and interaction networks of dendritic cell (DC) subtypes, macrophages, and cytotoxic T-cells within tertiary lymphoid structures (TLSs) and tumour islets.
  • Methods: Novel bioinformatics pipelines including STAT3D and standard tools (RStudio, Python) for dimensionality reduction and spatial cell-type analysis.
  • Integration: Spatial transcriptomics findings compared with spatial proteomics data (MACSima, PhenoImager HT) across HNC, lung, and breast cancer to characterise TLS composition and validate prior scRNA-seq findings.
2023 – 2024 BSc Thesis – Sickle Cell Disease University of Patras
  • Investigated physiological differences between red blood cell subpopulations in SCD patients, focusing on cell density, mitochondrial load, and oxidative stress.
  • Key findings: Higher hemolysis and oxidative stress linked to HbS polymerisation; impaired mitophagy driving mitochondrial retention; positive correlation between mitochondrial content, oxidative damage, and osmotic fragility.
  • Impact: Results inform understanding of distinct erythrocyte subpopulations in SCD pathophysiology and potential targeted therapeutic approaches — presented as a poster at the 30th EHA Congress, Milan.
2022 – 2025 Scientific Presentations & Posters Multiple International Conferences
  • Mitochondrial Retention in Sickle Cell Disease: Poster on retention mechanisms and their connection to disease heterogeneity — 30th EHA Congress, Milan.

  • Intracellular Calcium Regulation: Poster on Ca²⁺ regulation influencing resilience to corpuscular and redox stress — 75th Panhellenic HSBMB Conference.
